1. Management Consulting is…

"… a set of professional practices provided by one or more qualified persons to an organisation, aimed at improving its performance. This is achieved through the analysis of existing organisational problems and the development of plans for improvement."

- Paraphrased from industry definitions and ISO 20700 principles.

2. ‘Management Consulting’ vs. ‘Contracting’

While both roles provide external expertise, their focus and approach differ significantly. A consultant advises on 'what' to do and 'why', while a contractor is often hired to 'do' a specific task.

Management Consultant

  • Focus: Strategic advice, problem-solving, improving performance.
  • Role: Advisor, facilitator, analyst.
  • Deliverable: Recommendations, strategies, business cases, implementation plans.
  • Value: Provides external perspective, specialised expertise, and objectivity.
  • Goal: To enable the client to solve a problem or seize an opportunity.

Contractor / Freelancer

  • Focus: Executing specific tasks, filling a resource gap.
  • Role: Doer, temporary team member.
  • Deliverable: Completed work, code, designs, project milestones.
  • Value: Provides specific skills or extra capacity to deliver a defined output.
  • Goal: To complete a pre-defined scope of work.
